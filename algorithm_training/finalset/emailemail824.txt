*WINTER BREAK at HARVARD FOREST*

605Students from all concentrations and schools are encouraged to apply 
for the 2013 Winter Break program, "Reading and Conserving New England," 
which runs at the Harvard Forest in Petersham, Mass., from Jan. 20 to 
25. This interdisciplinary program, made possible at no cost to students 
by funds from the FAS dean, combines field-based activities on global 
change research topics with workshops in visual art, creative writing, 
and landscape design. The program is limited to 10 students. 
Applications 
<http://www.surveymonkey.com/checkrequest.aspx?aspxerrorpath=/s/KJFCJKT>are 
due Dec. 14, with notifications by Dec. 19.

More information is available on the program website 
<http://harvardforest.fas.harvard.edu/winter-break-week-harvard-forest>.

*SUMMER RESEARCH at HARVARD FOREST*

Summer REU Students on HikeThe 2013 Harvard Forest Summer Research (REU) 
Program is now accepting applications. This immersive, paid, 11-week 
research and education experience (May 20-Aug 2) is held on-site at 
Harvard's 3,500-acre forest lab and classroom in Petersham, Mass. 
  Project topics range from ecology and conservation to computer 
science, atmospheric science, social science, paleo-ecology, remote 
sensing, molecular biology, and biogeochemistry. Undergraduates and 
recent graduates from all U.S. colleges and universities are eligible to 
apply.

The application deadline is Feb. 1, 2013. Learn more 
<http://harvardforest.fas.harvard.edu/other-tags/reu>.
