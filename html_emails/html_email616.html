Hi Everyone, 

I hope you are all having great breaks.  I've participated in Relay for Life since my freshmen year here. It's a really great event. It's not a race. It's more like an overnight party where you get to hang with friends and donate to a great cause. It basically begins with an opening ceremony to kickoff events. Throughout the night participants have the option of walking around Gordon track (right across the river) or participating in night activities. It's really chill, and you can come and go. Or you can stay at the event all night. A ton of people camp out for the entire night. 

The Relay for Life committee at Harvard (http://main.acsevents.org/goto/HarvardBSA) works really hard to make it fun. I've provided a montage of .jpgs and .gifs to convey what it means to me. 


There are a ton of night time activities. While there is a formal time to walk in honor of cancer survivors, you are there to have fun!

They have sumo wrestling suits. I get the joy to taking down my friends every year.




There is so much food. The harvard cheerleaders sell cupcakes/cookies/treats that everyone loves. Companies come and give free samples. They even have a jello eating contest. People also "picnic", bring food, and share it. You could literally eat the whole night away:



Music blasts all night long thanks to a live DJ (he takes requests). They give you glow sticks, and you can move the party to the bounce house they have.


Convinced about joining yet?: http://main.acsevents.org/goto/HarvardBSA

There is a ratchett drag show. Everyone looks a hot mess and loves to do so. The winner even sashays away with a little prize. 




At the end of the night, I always wonder where the time went:





Stay up all night. Hang out with your friends. 
Make a difference. Laugh. Cry. Dance. Walk. 
Meet new friends. Give Hope. Fight Cancer. 
Join Relay For Life.

Please think about joining relay for life. It's an experience you'll never forget.
